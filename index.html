EMS MTSS Team Structure

Draft 2024–2025. Six meeting types coordinate universal, targeted, and intensive support.

Core MTSS
MTSS Team Meeting
📅 Monthly ⏱ 60 min
Led by: Principal & AP
Members: Instructional Coaches, School Psychologist, Counselors, Behavior Analyst, Special Ed Coordinator
Focus: School-wide data review, Tier 1 fidelity, resource allocation, intervention planning
Admin
Admin MTSS Meeting
📅 Weekly
Led by: Principal
Members: AP, Dean of Students
Focus: Urgent student concerns, discipline patterns, quick-response coordination
Intervention
Intervention Huddle
📅 Bi-weekly
Led by: Instructional Coaches
Members: Counselors, School Psychologist, Behavior Analyst
Focus: Tier 2 & 3 student progress, intervention fidelity, problem-solving for referred students
Advisory
Liaison Meeting
📅 Monthly
Led by: AP / Counseling
Members: Grade-level team leads, Community liaisons
Focus: Community partnerships, external agency coordination, family engagement
Discipline
Discipline Committee
📅 Monthly
Led by: Principal / AP
Members: Dean of Students, Counselors, Behavior Analyst
Focus: ODR (office discipline referral) data review, equity analysis, restorative practice follow-through
Competition
Clash of Classes Committee
📅 Monthly
Led by: CoC Coordinator / AP
Members: Squad General advisors, Coaches, Counselors
Focus: LRG point review, squad standings, upcoming events, Tier 1 positive behavior data integration, Trait Dough logging fidelity
Student Voice
Student Council / MLC
📅 Monthly
Led by: MLC Advisors
Members: Student representatives from all leadership tracks
Focus: Student feedback on school climate, Tier 1 initiatives, LRG integration, Clash of Classes planning

PowerSchool MTSS Process

How interventions are logged, communicated, and tracked in PowerSchool at EMS.

🔺
Find the MTSS Icon in PowerSchool
Look for the MTSS Pyramid icon on each student's page — located in the same icon row as Medical Alerts, Email Teachers, and Student Services. Click it to view active interventions and notes for that student.

How It Works

📋
1. Identify the Intervention
Use team/guidance meetings to discuss student needs and agree on an intervention. Common examples: schedule breaks, anytime passes, Compass Mark groups, point sheets, CICO, check-in with counselor.
🔐
2. Enter into PowerSchool
Counselors, HSVs, School Psychologists, and Admin are the only staff who can enter new interventions. After a team decision, one of these roles logs it in PS with details and notes.
📧
3. Teachers Are Notified
When a student's intervention is entered, all current teachers automatically receive an email from PowerSchool with the intervention details. No extra steps needed from classroom teachers.
📅
4. Weekly Check-In Emails
Each week, teachers receive a list of students with active interventions as an FYI. This keeps everyone informed without requiring additional meetings or communication.
✅
5. Closing an Intervention
When the team decides an intervention is no longer needed or effective, the MTSS Pyramid icon is removed from the student's profile. Data is stored historically and follows the student to the next building.
📁
6. Historical Record
All intervention data is stored historically in PowerSchool and transfers with the student to the next building — ensuring continuity of support across grade levels.
